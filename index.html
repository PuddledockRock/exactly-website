Technical Performance & Engineering QA

Geological Profile & Geotechnical Stability

The primary target resource at the Tilbuster Quarry asset consists of an abundant, highly silicified Hornfels metasediment (metamorphic classification: meta-feldspathic litharenite). Generated via contact metamorphism where heat from the proximal Triassic Tilbuster Granodiorite altered the dense Carboniferous Sandon Beds, this geological transformation has resulted in an exceptionally tough, fine-grained grey-to-black groundmass with extreme structural cohesiveness and high load-bearing capacity.

Comprehensive geotechnical profiling indicates the rock mass forms a highly stable, low-risk formation. The component clasts and lithic fragments are randomly oriented and tightly interlocked, ensuring the resource is free from localized planes of weakness or directional structural splitting.

  • Total Drilled Resource Volume: 2.76 million tonnes of continuous, unweathered hard rock.
  • Decomposition & Weathering Profile: Independently verified as Fresh under AS1726-2017 standards, demonstrating negligible alterability or mineralogical breakdown over multi-decade exposure horizons.

Geometric Conformity & Infrastructure Application

To satisfy strict Tier 1 pavement and concrete design mix requirements, the proposed site layout features a full mobile four-stage processing circuit incorporating primary jaw, secondary cone, and a specialized Vertical Shaft Impact (VSI) tertiary crusher operating with closed-circuit oversize return and triple-deck screening. The VSI circuit optimizes the particle shape of our 10mm and 20mm structural aggregate lines, dictating highly cubical, interlocked geometries that eliminate flaky or elongated shards. This directly optimizes paste efficiency and concrete compressive strength in heavy structural applications.

Asbestos-Free Mineralogical Certification: Independent petrographic microscopic thin-section analysis certifies that the target extraction mass is entirely free from asbestiform minerals.

Alkali-Reactivity Matrix: The resource is verified as innocuous regarding Alkali-Carbonate Reactivity (ACR) and carries a mild risk profile for Alkali-Silica Reactivity (ASR), an engineered variable that Tier 1 concrete technicians can easily mitigate using standard low-alkali cementitious formulations or supplementary cementitious materials (SCMs). Total free silica content is measured at 26.8%.

Prioritizing Historically Disturbed Footprints (Brownfield Asset Optimization)

Under modern environmental auditing matrices, greenfield sites that disrupt intact native vegetation or agricultural land score poorly. The proposed Tilbuster Quarry operation directly addresses this by optimizing a historically disturbed brownfield footprint:

  • Legacy Mining Footprint: The planned extraction zone sits entirely within a historical mining lease (ML1064) featuring a gold-mining legacy dating back to the 1860s. Sourcing from a site modified by industrial activity for over a century completely eliminates the need to clear pristine conservation lands or alienate high-value agricultural soil.
  • Environmental Legacy Protection: Our long-term objective is to systematically rehabilitate historical, un-remediated mining workings across the property, transforming legacy hazards into a stable, safe, and ecologically integrated landform.

Advanced Engineering for Low-Impact Neighborhood Protection

We recognise that local trust must be earned through verified operational performance. The quarry design incorporates robust engineering controls specifically selected to isolate operations and ensure the natural tranquility of the Puddledock Road locality remains completely unaffected. We do not expect our neighbors to simply take our word for it; we intend to demonstrate our low-impact metrics daily through transparent operational boundaries.

  • Subterranean Operational Design: The entire extraction and material-crushing circuit is engineered to occur exclusively within a subterranean pit floor dug well below the natural terrain level. The surrounding natural ridge lines act as a heavy, continuous acoustic and visual wall, completely blocking operations from public view and trapping sound waves.
  • Strict Operational Windows: To protect local evening amenity, the provisional schedule is strictly restricted to daytime hours (7:00 am to 6:00 pm Monday–Friday; 8:00 am to 1:00 pm Saturdays). The site will remain completely silent on Sundays and public holidays. Planned campaign blasting is confined to a restrictive weekday window of 9:00 am to 3:00 pm around 4 times per year.
  • Closed-Loop Hydrological Security: The proposed site model extracts zero water from local creeks, watercourses, or regional groundwater tables. A comprehensive system of internal diversion drains directs all operational runoff into dedicated on-site sediment basins, creating a closed-loop recycling system that completely isolates quarry water from downstream environments.
  • Resource Safeguards: The proposed layout maintains an unencumbered protective buffer zone setback from the buried municipal water pipeline easement that crosses the property margins from Puddledock Dam.
  • Air Quality Containment: To prevent dust migration across neighborhood boundaries, active water dust suppression will be deployed across internal tracking zones and work areas. All departing highway transport vehicles will operate under mandatory covered-load protocols and utilize automated rumble grids at the exit gate.

Governance, Risk Management & CoR

Proactive Archaeological Clearance

Our commitment to cultural heritage preservation is backed by empirical archaeological investigation. Rather than treating heritage as an afterthought, comprehensive field walking and surface visibility surveys were completed by specialized independent firms (Whincop Archaeology) with the active on-site participation of local Registered Aboriginal Parties (RAPs).

  • Aboriginal Heritage Impact Permits (AHIP): To guarantee full legal and ethical compliance, we are systematically progressing through the integrated development approvals framework to secure a formal Aboriginal Heritage Impact Permit (AHIP) under Section 90 of the National Parks & Wildlife Act 1974 prior to any physical ground disturbance. This ensures the controlled, respectful removal and preservation of these relics in full accordance with cultural protocols.

Strict Unexpected Finds Protocols

To completely de-risk field operations and insulate the project from sudden regulatory halts, the site will operate under a rigorous, mandatory Unexpected Finds Protocol developed in accordance with Heritage NSW guidelines:

  • Immediate Cessation Safeguards: In the unlikely event that any previously unrecorded subterranean Aboriginal object, tool, stone arrangement, or potential scar tree is encountered during bulk excavation or road building, all automated and physical works within a protective radius will instantly cease.
  • Professional Re-Evaluation Loops: The affected zone will be securely cordoned off, and the quarry manager will immediately re-engage a qualified archaeologist alongside local Aboriginal stakeholder groups and Heritage NSW representatives to inspect, log, and determine appropriate conservation management or salvage treatments before any works are cleared to resume.
  • Fulfilling Social Procurement Targets: Partnering with Puddledock Rock allows Tier 1 contractors to confidently satisfy complex social procurement KPIs mandated within major state infrastructure contracts, proving that their aggregate supply chain directly reinforces verified, respectful cultural heritage governance.
